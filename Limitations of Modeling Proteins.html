Limitations on Types of Proteins (top)

Most proteins can be modeled, but limitations or faults in the methods can prevent various types of proteins from being modeled realistically.  These limitations are:

  • Open shell systems are not allowed. This includes all proteins that have radicals, all photochemical reactions, and systems with open shell ions, such as [FeIII]3+, and [CoII]2+.
  • The Fe4S4 cluster geometry is not predicted well, so proteins containing this group should not be run.
