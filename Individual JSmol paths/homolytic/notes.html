Notes on homolytic fission

All files used here can be downloaded from homolytic.zip.  Due to small changes in precision and operating systems, a duplication of the following sequence might not be possible, however if the method used in generating the sequence is followed, similar results can be expected.

Files of type: "path UHF" - Stretching C57 - C54 bond until is breaks.
Click on "Script" to orientate system and display distances.
Model 10 is the highest energy system. In going to Model 11, the hydrogen on HOCO changes from being cis to being trans. At Model 23, the hydrogen on HOCO forms a hydrogen-bond with S60.
The geometry in the data-set is all Cartesian, except for C57, which is in internal coordinates.

Files of type: "path UHF reverse" - Compressing C57 - C54.
Click on "Script" to orientate system and display distances.
This system starts at the geometry in Model 13 in "path UHF" The ΔHf of Model 13 is -433.194 kcal mol-1. As the C - C distance is reduced, the energy drops steadily until Model 13.  Because the hydrogen on HOCO is now trans, the starting energy is -472.581 kcal mol-1, instead of -475.152 at the start of the "path UHF" trajectory.  Model 4, at -436.721 looks like a good candidate for a transition state calculation.

Files of type "path UHF TS" - refine the transition state.
Click on "Specific Script" to orientate system and display distances.
Now the C54 - C57 bond is at the transition-state geometry, and two hydrogen bonds are made between the HOCO and the rest of the system.

Files of type "UHF FORCE" - verify the transition state geometry.
This works out the vibrational frequencies of the transition state.  One "negative" vibration is expected, two, at  -139.1 and -83.6 cm-1 are found.
By adding keyword "ISOTOPE" a restart file is made.  This will save time in the next two calculations.

Files of type "IRC path 1" - Map the IRC from reactants to products for imaginary vibration 1.
Files of type "IRC path 2" - Map the IRC from reactants to products for imaginary vibration 2.
Both IRC's, from vibrations 1 and 2, produce more-or-less the same plot.  The starting point for the IRC is the separated HOCO, and the ending point is the similar to that in "path UHF reverse" i.e., the C-C bond is intact.
