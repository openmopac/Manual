That is, if a molecule, initially in its ground vibrational state, could be excited so that it vibrated at a given frequency, then that molecule could absorb a photon that vibrates at the same frequency. For a molecule, the force constants are obtained by diagonalization of the mass-weighted Hessian matrix. Most of the work in calculating vibrational frequencies is spent in constructing the Hessian.
