MACRO

In large molecules such as proteins atoms that are separated by a large distance, say 10 Angstroms or more, the NDDO approximation is not used, instead the only interaction that is used is the electrostatic energy arising from the atomic partial charge.  This makes the jobs run faster.

If MACRO is not used, then in large molecules the transition from NDDO to simple electrostatic point-charge is sudden.  This discontinuity introduces errors in the derivatives, that in turn cause problems for the optimizers.  The purpose of MACRO is to smooth the transition from NDDO to point-charge, so that the derivatives are not significantly affected. One side-effect of using MACRO is that the heat of formation is changed.  This change is not large, but is large enough to possibly cause problems with projects are are currently underway (August, 2020).

To avoid problems, add MACRO to all large jobs. 

Use of a keyword of this type is not a good solution to the problem.  A better solution would be to make the MACRO smoothing function an intrinsic part of all future methods.
