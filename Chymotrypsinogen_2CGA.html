Chymotrypsinogen 2CGA

The precursor or zymogen of chymotrypsin (CS) is chymotrypsinogen  (CG).  Chymotrypsinogen consists of a single chain (chain A) composed of 245 residues.  Comparing CG, which is enzymatically inactive, and CS, which is active, should provide insight into the origin of enzymic activity in CS.  The starting point for modeling CG is PDB entry 2CGA.   2CGA is a homodimer, so before any work was started one protein molecule, along with its associated water molecules, was deleted.  The remaining system was hydrogenated, residue Asp102 ionized, and the structure optimized. 

Formula used:  [C1127H1772N307O353S12]-.165(H2O)

Comparison of Chymotrypsinogen and Chymotrypsin

Chymotrypsinogen can be converted to CS by removing residues Gly12-Leu13-Ser14-Arg15 and Thr147-Asn148-Ala149. All of Ser11 except its nitrogen atom was also deleted. Tyr146 ends in -CO, presumably -CHO, and Asn150 starts with a nitrogen atom, presumably -NH2.
