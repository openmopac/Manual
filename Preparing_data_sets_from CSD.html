Preparing data-sets from CSD files

The Cambridge Structural Database, CSD, is a good source for geometries of crystalline organic compounds.  But frequently problems are encountered in converting a raw CSD file into a MOPAC data-set.  All such problems could be solved by a careful reading of the literature, but this is usually very time-consuming.  For convenience, then, a few worked examples will be given here. 

Common problems are:

Positional disorder, where a molecule in one location can be in two or more different orientations.  Examples: the 2-(2-pyridylamino)pyridinium cation in EMETAS

Positional disorder, where a small molecule is symmetry-related to an equivalent molecule, both sites having a 50% occupancy. Examples: A water molecule in EMETAS

EMETAS: Hexa-aqua-magnesium(ii) bis(2-(2-pyridyl)pyridinium) bis(adenosine-5'-triphosphate)-magnesium(ii) hexahydrate.
