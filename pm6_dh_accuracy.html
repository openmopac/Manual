Reference interaction energies (CCSD(T) from the  [CCSD(T)/complete basis set (CBS) limit] of the dispersion-bonded complexes.

Worked example:

A. PM6-DH Formic acid dimer ΔHf = -187.741
B. PM6-DH(1:5) Formic acid dimer ΔHf = -186.861
C. PM6 Formic acid dimer ΔHf = -180.615
D. PM6-DH two separated formic acid molecules ΔHf = -170.357
E. PM6-DH(1:5) two separated formic acid molecules ΔHf = -169.477
F. PM6 two separated formic acid molecules ΔHf = -169.477

Contribution to correction due to PM6-DH = A - C = -7.126 kcal/mol.
Interaction energy predicted using PM6 = C - F = -11.137 kcal/mol.
Interaction energy predicted using PM6-DH = A - D = -17.384 kcal/mol.
Interaction energy predicted using PM6-DH(1:5) = B - E = -17.384 kcal/mol.


Note 1: In D there are terms arising from atom pairs inside a formic acid that are missing in E.  This is because the PM6-DH terms in calculation E (like B) only involve inter-molecular contributions.  In calculations A and D all diatomic contributions are used.  This includes intra-molecular terms.

Note 2: In the Table, the interaction energy is the energy of the pair of molecules minus the energy of the well-separated pair, calculated using the same method.  It cannot be worked out using the other information in the Table.

Note 3: Because there are no intra- and no inter-molecular interactions in E, the heat of formation of E is identical to that of F.
