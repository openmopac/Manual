Effect of the correction made to the derivatives between Versions 20.254 and 20.288

(Back to Accuracy of PM7 and PM6-D3H4 )
(Home - Accuracy - Manual

In late 2020, severe errors were found in the PM6-D3H4 derivatives of the heat of formation with respect to geometry.  These only affected large systems, particularly enzymes and other proteins, and were traced to discontinuities in the energy - interatomic separation when atom-pairs were separated by several Ångstroms.  These errors were corrected, and the new derivatives allowed a much smaller final gradient to be obtained in optimized protein structures. For details of faults and how they were fixed, see Corrections.

Using the standard set of 77 proteins, for a total of 309,189 atoms, the effect of the corrections can be summarized as follows:

Average change in heat of formation per atom
for optimized geometry using old derivatives

+0.0212 kcal mol-1

 Average change in heat of formation per atom between old and new geometries using corrected gradients

-0.0772  kcal mol-1

One of the changes to the derivatives involved modifying the two-electron - two-center integrals.  This resulted in an average change in the heat of formation of the previously-optimized geometries of +0.0212 kcal mol-1 per atom. When the geometries were re-optimized, the heats of formation dropped by an average of 0.0772 kcal mol-1 per atom.

Evidence that the correction improves the results

  • Columns 8 and 9 shows the change in the heat of formation when the geometry was re-optimized using the corrected gradients. In Column 8 the ΔHf of the final geometry was calculated using the new gradients, and in Column 9 the old gradients were used in calculating the ΔHf.  In both cases the change in ΔHf was similar for each protein and in all cases this change was negative, indicating that the newly-optimized geometry was nearer to the minimum than before.

  • Columns 10 and 11 show the gradient norm of the optimized geometries.  The gradient norm when the old gradients were used was larger than when the new gradients were used.  This also indicates that the new gradients produce a better minimum.

  • Column 7 shows that correcting the gradients increases the ΔHf.  This change is a consequence of the first and third corrections, and has no other significance.

  • Two proteins, Bovine lactoferrin and Ricin A plus B, produced nonsensically large gradients when the old gradients were used.  This fault was rectified by the second correction.
