WARNING -- GEOMETRY IS NOT AT A STATIONARY POINT

Normally when a geometry optimization is complete, the geometry is is at a stationary point, that is, the forces acting on all atoms are below a preset limit.  There are three exceptions to this: (A) if some atoms are constrained (frozen or fixed), (B) a very rare case that occurs when internal coordinate gradients are small but the Cartesian derivatives are large, and (C) there is a bug in MOPAC  that causes the internal coordinates to be artificially small (no instance of this has occurred to date)  Whenever the calculated gradient norm, "GRADIENT NORM" in the output and ARC files, is small and the Cartesian gradient norm (not normally printed) is large, then the warning message: "WARNING -- GEOMETRY IS NOT AT A STATIONARY POINT" will be printed.  The action to take depends in which of the exception cases (A), (B), or (C) has occurred.

A:  If some atoms are constrained, for example by one or more optimization flags being set to zero, or by being a symmetry-dependent atom, then there is a high probability that the warning will be printed.  If the constraints are intended, then do nothing - the warning can be ignored.  If the constraints are incorrect, then correct them and re-run.

B: The simplest way to correct this error is to run the job in Cartesian coordinates.  To do this, add keywords OPT and XYZ.  An alternative would be to re-write the internal coordinates.  To do this, use keywords 0SCF and INT.

C: Please send the data set and output to openmopac|at|gmail.com
