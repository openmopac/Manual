(Modeling proteins)

Solvation

A realistic model of a protein is essential for studying biochemical processes.  If solvation is not used, then electrostatic effects, in particular those arising from ionized sites, on the surface of proteins would not be handled properly.  Adding implicit solvation by use of EPS=78.4 makes the computational model more realistic.  A realistic model is necessary because energy changes in biochemical processes are often very small - in the order of zero to a few Kcal/mol - and phenomena that depend on such small changes might not be reproduced properly if solvation was not used.

The cost, in terms of compute requirements, is not great, and the quality of the model is improved significantly.  Therefore, for all work involving proteins, use implicit solvation.

In the region of an active site, individual water molecules are important.  For this reason, explicit water molecules should be used, in addition to the use of implicit solvation.
