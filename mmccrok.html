MMCCROK

NDDO semiempirical methods predict some organic ring systems, such as cyclopentane, to be planar, although they are known to be puckered.  To correct for this, if MMCCROK is present, then a molecular mechanics correction can be applied.  The default form of this correction is:

DHf' = DHf + 4.0e-(angle/20.0)**2

where "angle" is the dihedral or torsion angle between four consecutive sp3 carbon atoms in a five membered ring.  The two constants, 4.0 and 20.0, can be changed by use of  MMCCR1 and MMCCR2, respectively. 

For planar cyclopentane, all the torsion angles are 180 degrees, therefore the effect of the correction is a maximum.

In cyclopentane, there are five sets of atoms like this, so five corrections would be made.
